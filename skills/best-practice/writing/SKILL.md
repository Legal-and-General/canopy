# Canopy Writing Principles — Best Practices

This skill provides guidance on tone of voice and content writing for applications built with Canopy.

Apply this skill when writing any UI text: headings, body copy, labels, error messages, or button text.

---

## Brand Personality

Write in a way that reflects three core traits:

- **Bright** — bring intelligence and energy; demonstrate expertise in accessible language.
- **Calm** — keep it simple, concise, and confident; let the facts speak.
- **Real** — be down to earth, warm, and transparent; write like you speak.

---

## Content Principles

1. **Lead with the benefit** — tell users what's in it for them early on.
2. **Understand your audience** — explain acronyms, avoid jargon, write at reading age 9.
3. **Active, not passive** — "The system updated your address" not "Your address has been updated".
4. **Draw pictures with words** — prefer concrete objects over abstract ideas.
5. **Structure your thoughts** — order messages logically before you write.
6. **Edit and simplify** — cut unnecessary words; avoid long sentences.
7. **Use "you"** — prefer "you" over "we" to involve the reader.
8. **Write like you speak** — use contractions ("You're" not "You are"), avoid clichés.

---

## Practical Rules

### Headings and Titles

- Set expectations — titles should describe what the content contains and how it helps.
- Keep titles short and actionable: "How do I update my address online".

### Button Text

- Use descriptive verbs: **Accept**, **Create**, **Delete**, **Save**, **Update**.
- Avoid vague terms: **Yes**, **No**, **OK**, **Click here**.

### Link Text

- Use words that describe the destination or action: "Update my address", "View my policy documents".
- Never write "click here" or "find out more" as link text.

### Error Messages

- Be specific about what went wrong and what the user needs to do to fix it.
- Use active, direct language without blame.

### Paragraphs and Body Copy

- Keep each paragraph short and to the point.
- Break content into bite-sized chunks; users scan rather than read.
- Use subheadings, bullet points, and whitespace to aid scanning.

---

## Dos and Don'ts

### Do

1. **Do** focus on the customer's goal before writing.
2. **Do** use words that customers themselves would use.
3. **Do** use active, confident language: "you should", "we recommend".
4. **Do** keep body text scannable with short paragraphs and subheadings.

### Don't

1. **Don't** use internal jargon or technical acronyms without explanation.
2. **Don't** use passive voice when active is possible.
3. **Don't** use vague button or link labels ("Click here", "Find out more").
4. **Don't** write long paragraphs that are difficult to scan.
